# The Pyth Community Maestro

**Role:** Community Lead + BD, Pyth Network

I run the community and BD function at Pyth Network — the first-party oracle network delivering 400+ price feeds across 100+ blockchains, powering 60% of perpetuals protocols on-chain. The thesis is simple: Pyth is building the **price layer for global finance**, and the $50B+ financial data industry that Bloomberg and Reuters have dominated for decades is being quietly disrupted underneath it.

My job is to make sure the 500K+ community around this network is the moat — not a marketing funnel, not a vanity metric, but a real cultural machine that compounds over decades.

---

## The Frame

**Maestro, not manager.**

A manager keeps a schedule. A maestro reads every instrument in the room, knows which one is about to go flat, and conducts the whole thing into something that sounds like one song.

Community at this scale isn't one program — it's a dozen moving parts (Discord, Telegram, content creators, governance, ambassadors, partner protocols, hackathons, BD pipelines, incentive systems) that all have to stay in time with each other. My job is the tempo. Recognize the early contributor before someone else does. Kill the program that's drifting. Keep the flywheel spinning when the narrative cycle turns against us. Ship the system that makes 100 people feel like 1,000.

**Communities beat VCs when designed correctly.** That's not a slogan — it's the thesis I've been operating under since 2024 (see [Why Communities Beat VCs](../writings/why-communities-beat-vcs/why-communities-beat-vcs)) and it's what the Pyth community work is built around.
