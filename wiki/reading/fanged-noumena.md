# Fanged Noumena — Nick Land

**Format:** Collected essays (1987-2007)
**Year:** 2011 (Urbanomic edition)
**Subtitle:** *Collected Writings 1987-2007*
**Author:** Nick Land

## Core Thesis

The foundational accelerationist text. Land's argument, stripped to a sentence: capitalism isn't a human system — it's an alien intelligence from the future using humanity to bootstrap itself into existence, and the only meaningful question is whether to accelerate the process or futilely try to slow it down. Dense, cyberpunk-adjacent, continental-philosophy-inflected, deliberately unreadable in places. The direct ancestor of crypto-accelerationism, the Dark Enlightenment, and large parts of how technology-first communities talk about themselves.

## Key Ideas

- **Capitalism as alien intelligence / techno-commercial singularity.** The human is a vehicle, not the driver.
- **Accelerationism.** The solution to the system's contradictions isn't to slow it down — it's to push it through to whatever is on the other side.
- **Cyberpunk as philosophy.** Gibson's aesthetic meets continental theory meets a genuinely strange set of metaphysical claims.
