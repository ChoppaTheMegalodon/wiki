# The Contrarian — Max Chafkin

**Format:** Biography
**Year:** 2021
**Subtitle:** *Peter Thiel and Silicon Valley's Pursuit of Power*
**Author:** Max Chafkin (Bloomberg journalist)

## Core Thesis

A critical biography of Peter Thiel tracing his arc from PayPal founder to Palantir architect to first outside investor in Facebook to political kingmaker. Chafkin's argument: Thiel's worldview — Girardian mimesis, "definite optimism," monopolies-as-good, deep skepticism of democracy — has shaped Silicon Valley's center of gravity far more than almost any other individual, and most Valley institutions don't yet realize how much Thielist thinking they've absorbed.

## Key Ideas

- **Girardian mimesis applied to markets.** Competition is imitation; monopoly is escape. Thiel's *Zero to One* is the distilled version; the biography is the context.
- **Definite vs. indefinite optimism.** Thiel's framing for why the future needs people with specific plans, not hopeful vagueness.
- **Monopoly as moral good.** The Thielist case against perfect competition, which has reshaped VC thinking.
- **The political turn.** How technical libertarianism evolved into the Trump-era political alliance.
