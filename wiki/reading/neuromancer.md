# Neuromancer — William Gibson

**Format:** Novel (currently reading)
**Year:** 1984
**Author:** William Gibson

## Core Thesis

Not a thesis so much as a *substrate*. Neuromancer is the cyberpunk ur-text — the novel that coined "cyberspace" and "the matrix," defined the aesthetic of dense-networked-corporate-dystopia, and gave crypto-native culture almost all of its visual and imaginative vocabulary decades before crypto existed. It's not a book you read for lessons; it's a book you read to understand the cultural DNA of the space you're already working in.

## Key Ideas

- **"Cyberspace. A consensual hallucination experienced daily by billions of legitimate operators..."** — the working definition of every networked reality since, including crypto. Not a place so much as a shared mental model made operational.
- **Corporate power exceeds state power.** Gibson's "zaibatsus" run the world. Sovereignty has leaked from governments into networks of capital and technology. Prescient beyond comfort.
- **Identity is fluid and constructed.** Characters inhabit pseudonyms, bodies, constructs. Reputation travels across networks in ways that don't map to physical identity. The pattern we now call "pseudonymous participation" in crypto.
- **The network is an ecology, not a tool.** AIs, humans, corporations, and subcultures all interact as actors in a living system. The metaphor of "living machines" I use for community philosophy is downstream of this.
- **Case and Molly as archetypes** — the hacker and the street-sovereign. Every crypto community has both.
