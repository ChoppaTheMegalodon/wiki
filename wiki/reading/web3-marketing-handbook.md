# Web3 Marketing Handbook — Amanda Cassatt

**Format:** Book
**Year:** 2023
**Author:** Amanda Cassatt (formerly led marketing at ConsenSys)
**Subtitle:** *Make Your Business Unstoppable With the New Internet Revolution*
**Where to find it:** [Amazon AU](https://www.amazon.com.au/Web3-Marketing-Handbook-Internet-Revolution/dp/1394171951)

## Core Thesis

Web3 marketing isn't Web2 marketing with a token bolted on. It's a categorically different discipline — community-first, narrative-driven, token-aligned — where the customer and the marketer and the shareholder are often the same person. Treat the community as the distribution channel and the tokenholder as the co-owner, and the playbook reorganizes itself.

## Key Ideas

- **Community is the marketing.** In Web3, your community is simultaneously your customers, your distribution, your validators, and — via tokens — your investors. Confusing these roles is the source of most Web3 marketing failures.
- **Narrative > product.** Tokens amplify narratives faster than products amplify utility. The protocol with the better story, not necessarily the better tech, wins the attention war. But the narrative has to be real — Web3 is less forgiving of manufactured spin than Web2 is.
- **Tokens are both asset and advertisement.** Holding a token is a public statement. That makes every holder a walking marketing surface — and a walking risk, if the narrative breaks.
- **GTM maps: community-led → developer-led → institution-led.** Sequence matters. Skipping community to go straight to institutions is the default Web3 failure mode.
- **Practical playbooks for:** naming, tokenomics-as-marketing, Discord/Telegram/Twitter strategy, NFTs as membership, DAO participation design.

## Where I Disagree / Would Extend

[TBD — pushback. Early:
- Cassatt is strong on the what (community-first, narrative-driven) but light on the how of actually sustaining a community past the launch phase. The book reads as a GTM manual more than a stewardship manual. What happens on day 400 when the narrative has dulled?
- Doesn't engage seriously with cultural capture, governance hijacking, or extractive community members. The sunny case only.
- "Token as advertisement" is true and under-interrogated — what happens when your tokenholders become *hostile* marketers during a drawdown? The handbook doesn't answer.
- Maps to crypto-native audiences well; less useful for protocols trying to reach institutional / non-crypto-native users (which is exactly Pyth's adjacent problem).]
