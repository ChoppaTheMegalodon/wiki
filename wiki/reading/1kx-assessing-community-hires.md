## Core Thesis

Community hiring in token networks is a different job than community hiring in Web2 companies — the role involves coordinating contributors you don't manage, navigating cultural contexts where "best practices" often backfire, and operating in public with unusual stakes. Most hires fail because the evaluation looked for Web2 credentials (brand marketing, event management, customer success) instead of the actual competencies that matter in crypto (cultural fluency, builder-respect, narrative judgment, autonomous execution).

## Key Ideas

- **Cultural fluency predicts performance.** Does the candidate already have a home in crypto culture, or are they translating in real time? The latter almost never works at scale — the community can tell.
- **Hire builders, not broadcasters.** The community lead who has shipped anything (a tool, a bot, a post, a contribution) outperforms the one with a larger follower count. Proof of work beats proof of presence.
- **Autonomous execution matters more than process.** Token network community leads don't have managers over their shoulder. If the candidate needs to be told what to do, they'll underperform regardless of credentials.
- **Judgment > skills.** Crypto moves too fast for playbooks. Hire for judgment — how the candidate reasons about edge cases, not how they describe their past tactics.
- **The tell: who do they already know, and what do those people say about them?** Cultural embeddedness leaves a trace.

## Where I Disagree / Would Extend

[TBD — my pushback. Early:
- The essay is strong on "how to hire" but light on "how to keep." The retention problem is harder than the acquisition problem in crypto community roles. A separate essay is needed on when to let a community lead graduate out of the role rather than stay too long.
- The "judgment > skills" framing is right but under-specified — *how* do you evaluate judgment in a 30-minute interview? The essay doesn't give a practical technique.
- Doesn't address the structural issue: token networks often have no hiring manager with enough context to actually run this kind of evaluation. Who assesses the community lead when nobody above them has done the work?]
