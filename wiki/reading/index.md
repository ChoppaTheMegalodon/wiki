# Reading List

What I've read, what I'm reading, what I keep coming back to. Organized around the four things I actually think about: **Community, Cults, Communication, Culture.**

A working document. Each entry below is its own page with a short synthesis, where it shows up in my own work, and — more importantly — where I'd disagree or extend the author's argument. If a book is here, it's because it genuinely changed how I think about something, not because it looks good on a shelf.

The four themes overlap. A lot. That's the point — the interesting work is in the overlap.

---

## Community

The core discipline. How groups of people form, sustain themselves, die, and occasionally become something that outlasts anyone running them.

- [[get-together|Get Together]] — Bailey Richardson et al. The practitioner's handbook for starting a community from zero. People & Company, 2019.
- [[network-state|The Network State]] — Balaji Srinivasan, 2022. Community as proto-nation-state; crowdfunded sovereignty as a startup path.
- [[1kx-hard-thing-communities|The Hard Thing About Communities]] — pet3rpan / 1kx. Riffs on Ben Horowitz. The unglamorous middle — cultural drift, extractive members, founder dependence.
- [[1kx-operational-autonomy|How Communities Gain Operational Autonomy Over Time]] — pet3rpan / 1kx. The maturation arc: founder-dependent → community-dependent.
- [[1kx-assessing-community-hires|How to Assess New Community-Building Hires]] — pet3rpan / 1kx. Hiring framework for token networks. Why credentials don't predict community judgment.
- [[1kx-grant-programs|Guide on Launching Grant Programs]] — pet3rpan / 1kx. The default crypto grants playbook.
- [[pet3rpan-community-1473|pet3rpan — community observation (Dec 2021)]]
- [[pet3rpan-community-1403|pet3rpan — community observation (June 2021)]]

## Cults

Mass movement mechanics. Why people believe, what makes belief sticky, why the best communities borrow cult structure without the pathology.

- [[network-state|The Network State]] — Balaji Srinivasan. The network state is arguably a cult with better UX. Read as a cult-construction manual.
- [[1kx-hard-thing-communities|The Hard Thing About Communities]] — touches on cultural capture, when a community becomes dogma instead of dialog.

*More to add. Currently reading candidates below.*

## Communication

How ideas travel through networks. Memes, narratives, voice, the mechanics of making something stick.

- [[web3-marketing-handbook|Web3 Marketing Handbook]] — Amanda Cassatt, 2023. Web3 marketing is fundamentally different from Web2 — community-first, token-aligned, narrative-driven. The practitioner's guide.

*More to add.*

## Culture

The weirder, slower, higher-leverage layer. What people actually care about when they're not working. Where meaning lives.

- [[neuromancer|Neuromancer]] — William Gibson, 1984. (Currently reading.) Cyberpunk founding text. Coined "cyberspace." The aesthetic and imaginary substrate underneath almost all crypto culture.
- [[meditations|Meditations]] — Marcus Aurelius, ~180 AD. Stoic philosophy as personal journal. Discipline, endurance, what you control vs. what you don't. Personal operating system.
- [[network-state|The Network State]] — Balaji Srinivasan. Cultural argument more than a political one. How a tribe becomes a people becomes a nation.

## How This List Works

Each entry is its own page. Pages follow a consistent pattern — core thesis, key ideas, where-it-shows-up-in-my-own-work, where-I-disagree, related. See [[get-together]] for the shape.

**The "Where I Disagree / Would Extend" section is the whole point.** Anyone can summarize a book. What's worth reading is the pushback — what the author missed, got wrong, or didn't push far enough. That's where the synthesis lives.
