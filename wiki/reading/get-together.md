# Get Together — Bailey Richardson et al.

**Format:** Book
**Year:** 2019
**Authors:** Bailey Richardson, Kevin Huynh, Kai Elmer Sotto (People & Company; Bailey was on Instagram's early community team)
**Where to find it:** [Booktopia](https://www.booktopia.com.au/get-together-bailey-richardson/book/9781732265196.html)

## Core Thesis

Communities are built by finding people who care about the same thing, giving them rituals and reasons to show up, and deepening those relationships over time — not by extracting attention from an audience. The "Get Together → Build Together → Go Together" arc: find your people, make rhythms, scale through them rather than past them.

## Key Ideas

- **Communities are participatory, audiences aren't.** The line between the two is whether people show up for each other or just for you. Every program you ship has to invite participation, not consumption.
- **Find the people who care before you build for them.** Most "community" launches skip this — they build a Discord and hope. The successful ones start by finding the ten people who already care, deeply.
- **Rituals are the load-bearing structure.** Recurring rhythms (weekly shows, monthly meetups, seasonal awards) do more for community stickiness than any feature launch. The ritual is the product.
- **Identify and invest in your super-contributors.** The top 1% do 80% of the value creation. Name them, resource them, give them authority.
- **Go together, not ahead.** Growth comes from deepening existing members' agency, not chasing new ones. The same 100 deeply engaged people produce more than 10,000 shallow ones.
