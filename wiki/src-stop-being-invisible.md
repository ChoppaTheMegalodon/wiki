# Source: Stop Being Invisible — Gearside

**Format:** YouTube video, 12:10
**Speakers:** Charlie (host) + Jonno
**Channel:** Gearside
**Published:** 2026-04-09
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UlTGbJpO-fo

## TL;DR

Core thesis: most professionals pay an **"invisibility tax"** — when only your boss knows you're good at your job, you earn less and get fewer promotions. The fix is a public portfolio + point of view. Old-school "head down, loyalty, pay rise" social contract is broken; you need to put yourself out there, own a niche, and be authentically yourself online. AI slop raises the value of authenticity rather than lowering it. Smallest first step: register a real domain, build a site, publish your first post.

## Key Arguments

1. **The Invisibility Tax exists whether you see it or not.** If no one outside your org chart knows you exist, you're structurally underpriced. Wage growth in Australia/the West is flat; loyalty no longer compounds. See [[invisibility-tax]].

2. **Portfolio applies to everyone now, not just designers.** Nurses can write about ward experiences. Tradies can document projects. Knowledge workers write case studies or own a narrow topic. Proof-of-thought is the new proof-of-work. See [[portfolio-as-proof]].

3. **Pick a specific niche and aggregate the news/ideas around it.** Become "the person who cares most about memory chips" (or solar, or oracles, or whatever). Specificity beats breadth. Reputation compounds.

4. **The authenticity paradox.** The more you're yourself — jokes, takes, blurred work/life line — the more employable you become. Counter-intuitive because LinkedIn culture punishes it, but the market rewards it. AI-generated slop makes human voice scarcer and more valuable.

5. **Hiring manager's view:** most resumes go straight to bin. A custom website + a point of view = top 1% of 1% of candidates. Bar is shockingly low because most people don't do the basics.

6. **Smallest first step, quoted literally:**
   > "Register your own domain name, set up a website — you can easily just get ChatGPT or Claude to build you one. That's a good skill to learn. The second thing to do, and this is obviously harder, is actually do your first post. That's probably the threshold that people find most uncomfortable."

7. **Tall poppy note (relevant to Australian audiences):** The cultural instinct to pull others down is fading. Social contract is broken enough that "no one's coming to save them" — people are putting themselves out there more. If family/friends aren't happy for you, consider whether you're spending time with the right people.

## Quotable Lines

- "The invisibility tax is what happens where the only person who knows you're only good at your job is your boss."
- "Buy a domain name, act like an adult."
- "The people that post the crazy bit clever takes, or the people that are funny on those platforms — they stand out like dog's balls."
- "Bring your full self to work, then post it on the internet."
- "Close this tab, get out there, start posting. It's not cringe anymore."
